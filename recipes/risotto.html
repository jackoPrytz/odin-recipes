Mushroom Risotto

Creamy mushroom risotto

Risotto (/rɪˈzɒtoʊ/, Italian: [riˈzɔtto, -ˈsɔt-], from riso meaning "rice")[1] is a northern Italian rice dish cooked with broth until it reaches a creamy consistency. The broth can be derived from meat, fish, or vegetables. Many types of risotto contain butter, onion, white wine, and Parmigiano-Reggiano. It is one of the most common ways of cooking rice in Italy. Saffron was originally used for flavour and its signature yellow colour.[2

Ingredients

  • 1 Handful fresh parsley
  • 100g butter
  • 1 brown onion
  • Portobello mushrooms
  • 1 glove galic
  • 20g parmesan cheese
  • 600ml chicken broth
  • 200g Arborio rice
  • Sweet vermouth

Steps

  1. Finely dice both the garlic and onion and in a mdeium sauce pan cook until golden brown
  2. Slice mushrooms finely and add to onions and cook until brown, add sweet vermouth and reducedown
  3. Once all moisture is gone add 1/2 a cup of chicken broth and reduce before adding all the rice.
  4. Continuously stir rice adding 1/2 cup of liquid when it begins to stick to the bottom of the pan.-Repeat this step until all broth is used.
  5. Add butter and half of the parmesan and parsley to the pan and stir well.
  6. serve into small bowls seasoning well with salt and pepper and garnish with the rest of the parmesan and parsley.
